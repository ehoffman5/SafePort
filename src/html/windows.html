How to close open ports on Windows OS




Before we begin, we want to remind you to not get discouraged. This guide was specifically made to be accessible to a person of ANY experience level.



Step 1: Identify the Port You Want to Close


As stated above, not every open port needs to be closed.



Step 2: Navigate to Your Firewall


Press the Start button in the bottom left corner of your screen. Click the Control Panel (for newer versions of Windows, you can use the search bar to search for it). Once in the Control Panel, either click “Windows Defender Firewall” or use the search bar in the top right to search for it.







Step 3: Navigate to Inbound Rules


Once in the "Windows Defender Firewall" window, click on “Advanced Settings” and then click on “Inbound Rules” on the top left.





Step 4: Create a New Rule


Either right click on “Inbound Rules” and press “New Rule”, or press the "New Rule" button on the top right.



Step 5: Click the Port Button


Press the “Port” button and then click next.





Step 6: Specify Your Port


Make sure the “TCP” and “Specific Local Ports” boxes are checked. Type in the port or ports you want to close into the textbox. For multiple ports, enter the ports as a comma separated list.





Step 7: Click "Block the Connection"


Click “Block the Connection” and then press next.





Step 8: Ensure All 4 Boxes are Checked


Check all 4 boxes and then press next.





Step 9: Name the Rule and Press Finish


Once you press finish, the port will be closed!
